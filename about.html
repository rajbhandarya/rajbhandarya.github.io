Campus Care Project

Teammates: Ella Visconti, Mustafe Osman, Conrado Peña Lemes

Campus Care is an all-encompassing and interactive mental health hub for Carleton students. It contains information regarding counseling services, affiliated offices, upcoming wellness events, critical mental health awareness information, and contacts. It is designed to be interactive so users can customize these resources towards their personal experiences and mental health needs.

From the requirements elicitation, I learned to include descriptions of what mental health is and why it is important, as this is helpful for people to think more about in their mental health journey, although this is often overlooked. I included this as an informational piece on the front webpage. Additionally, I included key Carleton mental health resources such as TimelyCare that was recently launched, SHAC counseling services, and student-led peer support groups that were mentioned during the interview.


Requirements Generation

Common Q&As
Description: List of commonly asked questions and misconceptions with detailed answers below each based on the user-selected question.
Usage: The website includes a “Help” section which includes FAQs, points of contact, and troubleshooting.

Resource Directory
Description: Comprehensive guide of all mental health and wellness resource options with links to all related organizations at Carleton and Student Wellness Directory.
Usage: Included under the “Resources” tab is a portal to all relevant mental health resources which the user can then search for specific resources using keywords or click on a resource to delve deeper into that resource.

Counseling Options/Scheduling
Description: Comprehensive list of what counseling services are available, how to access/schedule them, and other relevant information.
Usage: Through the resource directory, you are able to immediately access counseling services and from there, are able to schedule a counseling appointment through either SHAC, TimelyCare, or group counseling sessions.


UX Design & Analysis Process
In order to understand more about user needs and gaps in addressing these needs on campus, we conducted semi-structured and fictional inquiry interviews for two main sets of stakeholders: 1. Carleton students who may use the resources, and 2. students, faculty, and staff that manage the resources or have direct knowledge of mental health needs on campus.
In order to analyze the interviews, we axial coded each of the transcripts and created an affinity diagram, piecing together important themes from stakeholder statements in the interviews. From this affinity diagram, we were able to then determine key words/phrases that help describe common themes regarding mental health resources on campus that the stakeholders identified to help map a better conceptual model of our issue and what steps needed to be taken for our website. Some key ideas we were able to draw from these interviews included centrality of SHAC counseling services, information fragmentation and reach, and gaps in knowledge of available resources that address their needs. Thus, from these thematic insights, we were able to agree on the main requirements stated above (Common Q&As, Resource Directory, and Counseling Options/Scheduling) for the website/application to properly address these existing concerns.

Wellness Calendar: How users can interact with the wellness calendar, viewing events and activities related to mental health on campus.

Resource Portal: How users can navigate the mental health resource portal and access related services on campus.

Q&As/Help Section: An overview of the Help section, including FAQs and troubleshooting for students needing assistance.

Scheduling Counseling Service: How users can schedule counseling services through the platform.
